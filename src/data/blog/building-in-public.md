There's something uncomfortable about sharing work before it's finished.

The instinct is to wait until it's polished - until the idea is fully formed, the code is clean, and the design is pixel-perfect. But that instinct keeps things hidden too long, and hidden things don't get better from feedback they never receive.

## Why build in public?

A few reasons, in rough order of importance:

1. **It forces clarity.** Writing about what I'm doing, even imperfectly, requires understanding it well enough to explain it. That process itself sharpens the thinking.

2. **It creates accountability.** Declaring intent publicly makes it real. Projects with an audience, even a small one, get finished.

3. **It compounds.** Each post is a small asset. Over time, a body of honest work is more valuable than any single polished piece.

## The discomfort is the point

The resistance I feel before publishing - *is this good enough, is this obvious, what if I'm wrong* - is almost always a signal that I should publish anyway.

The things that feel too simple to write about are often the things others are quietly wondering about. The things that feel too unfinished often benefit most from early feedback.

## What I've changed

I used to wait. Now I ship early and revise publicly. The quality of the work has improved, and unexpectedly, so has my confidence in it.
